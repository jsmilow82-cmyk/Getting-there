Speaking 1: Lessons 1–3

Running into an Old Friend

You’ve just moved to a new neighborhood in your city. It’s your first day in the neighborhood and you are walking around to check it out.

You run into an old friend who has lived in the neighborhood for a long time.

Your job: greet your friend, ask questions, and keep the conversation going.

Page 1 / 4

Neighborhood vibe

Ask about the neighborhood in general.

Try to use:

  • lively
  • scene (restaurant / bar / music / nightlife / cafe / food, etc.)
  • remote
  • run-down
  • check out

Example prompts:

  • What’s the neighborhood like?
  • Is it lively or more quiet?
  • Is there a good restaurant or nightlife scene?
  • Are there any places I should check out?
Page 2 / 4

Safety & crime

Ask about safety in the neighborhood.

Try to use:

  • break in
  • break-in
  • break into
  • mug / mugging
  • safe
  • safety
  • burglary

Example prompts:

  • Is the neighborhood safe?
  • Have there been any break-ins or burglaries?
  • Do people ever get mugged around here?
  • Do you feel safe walking here at night?
Page 3 / 4

Places & plans

Ask about establishments and make plans to visit one together.

Try to use:

  • on the corner
  • around the corner
  • bodega
  • grab a… (coffee / sandwich / drink, etc.)
  • I’ve been dying for…

Example prompts:

  • Are there any good cafes or restaurants around here?
  • Is there a bodega on the corner?
  • Want to grab a coffee?
  • I’ve been dying for a good coffee / croissant / burger.
Page 4 / 4

Full role play

Now do the full role play from start to finish.

  • Greet your friend
  • Ask about the neighborhood vibe
  • Ask about safety
  • Ask about places to go
  • Make plans together

Challenge: Speak for 3–5 minutes without stopping.
